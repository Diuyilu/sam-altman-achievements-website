Sam Altman

Samuel Harris "Sam" Altman (born April 22, 1985) is an American entrepreneur, investor, and the CEO of OpenAI. He is a leading figure in the AI boom. He previously served as the president of Y Combinator.

Learn More

Early Life & Education

Altman was born in Chicago, Illinois, and grew up in St. Louis, Missouri. He received his first computer, an Apple Macintosh, at age eight, which sparked his interest in technology.

He attended John Burroughs School and later studied computer science at Stanford University for two years before dropping out to pursue his entrepreneurial ventures.

Career Journey

Loopt

At 19, Altman co-founded Loopt, a location-based social networking mobile app. He raised over $30 million in venture capital. In 2012, Loopt was acquired by Green Dot Corporation for $43.4 million.

Y Combinator

Altman became the president of Y Combinator in 2014. Under his leadership, YC expanded significantly, aiming to fund 1,000 companies annually. He introduced programs like YC Continuity and YC Research.

YC funded notable companies like Airbnb, Dropbox, Reddit, and Instacart during his tenure. He stepped down in 2019.

OpenAI Leadership

Altman co-founded OpenAI in 2015 and became its CEO. His leadership is marked by a visionary approach to achieving Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) for the benefit of humanity.

He has been instrumental in the development of groundbreaking projects like ChatGPT, DALL-E, and GPT-4.

Key Achievements & Impact

  • Co-founding and selling Loopt for $43.4 million.
  • Leading Y Combinator's significant growth and nurturing numerous successful startups.
  • Spearheading OpenAI's development of advanced AI models like GPT-3 and ChatGPT, which have revolutionized the AI industry.
  • Founding Worldcoin, a biometric cryptocurrency project.
  • Advocating for responsible AI development and engaging with policymakers globally.

Controversies & Challenges

Sam Altman's career has not been without challenges and controversies:

  • His temporary ouster from OpenAI in November 2023 by the board, who cited a lack of candor.
  • A lawsuit from Elon Musk regarding OpenAI's shift from a non-profit mission.
  • Allegations regarding the use of a voice similar to Scarlett Johansson's for an AI assistant without her consent.

Future Outlook

Altman's future plans are centered on advancing AI towards AGI. He envisions AI agents becoming integral to the workforce, accelerating scientific discovery, and revolutionizing human-computer interaction.

He is focused on ensuring these advancements are developed and deployed safely and for the benefit of all.
